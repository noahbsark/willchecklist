Affiliate & Advertising Disclosure

How monetization may appear on the site and how it should be labeled.

Our disclosure principle

Will Checklist may earn money from affiliate links, sponsored placements, lead forms, display advertising, newsletter sponsorships, or digital products. Any monetization should be clearly labeled and should not change the educational purpose of the content.

Affiliate links

Some links may become affiliate links. If you click an affiliate link and make a purchase or submit information, the site may earn a commission or referral fee at no additional cost to you. Affiliate placeholders currently use labels such as AFFILIATE_LINK_EXAMPLE_1 and must be replaced only after you join an approved program.

Advertising

Display ad slots are included as placeholders. Replace ADSENSE_PUBLISHER_ID_PLACEHOLDER only after approval by an advertising provider and after confirming the ad experience remains clear and non-intrusive.

No fake reviews or rankings

We do not claim that a product or service is “best” based on fake testing. Comparison pages use neutral language such as “worth considering,” “may fit,” and “compare options” unless future evidence supports stronger claims.

Lead forms

Lead forms should only be connected to reputable services with clear consent language. Do not sell or transfer sensitive estate planning details without explicit consent and appropriate safeguards.

Editorial independence

Monetization should remain secondary to usefulness. If a recommendation cannot be explained clearly and honestly, it should not be added.

Questions

Contact us through the contact page with disclosure questions.
