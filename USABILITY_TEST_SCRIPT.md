# WillChecklist.com Usability Test Script

Use this when you give the site to real users. Do not coach them unless they are stuck.

## Test goal
See whether a first-time visitor can understand the site, start the correct flow, avoid entering sensitive information, and reach a useful summary or printable packet.

## Setup
- Use the latest `/site` folder.
- Tell the tester to use fake example information only.
- Ask them to think out loud.
- Do not explain the site first.

## Task 1: First impression
Open the homepage. Ask:
- What do you think this site helps you do?
- What would you click first?
- Is anything confusing or concerning?

Pass signal: they understand this is an organizing tool, not a legal document creator.

## Task 2: Start
Ask the tester to begin. Watch whether they choose the setup wizard, continue page, or dashboard.

Pass signal: they can start without needing instructions.

## Task 3: Safety
Ask: What should you avoid typing into this site?

Pass signal: they mention passwords, SSNs, full account numbers, access codes, private keys, seed phrases, or confidential details.

## Task 4: Complete one step
Ask the tester to complete one tool using fake information.

Watch for:
- Do they understand checkboxes and note fields?
- Do helper messages feel clear or annoying?
- Do they know progress is local to the browser?

## Task 5: Continue
After one tool, ask: What would you do next?

Pass signal: they notice the next-step panel or Continue page.

## Task 6: Summary
Ask them to find a way to create or print a summary.

Pass signal: they find Summary Builder, Dashboard, or printable PDFs without help.

## Final questions
- What was easiest?
- What was confusing?
- What did you ignore?
- What made you trust or distrust the site?
- What would you expect to happen after clicking a form or download button?
- Would you use this again later? Why or why not?
