Editorial Policy & Sources

How Will Checklist keeps estate planning preparation content useful, cautious, and transparent.

Will Checklist publishes general educational checklists and organization tools for U.S. adults who are preparing to make or update estate planning documents. The site is designed to help visitors gather information, compare practical options, and prepare better questions.

Important limitation: this site does not provide legal, tax, financial, medical, or safety advice. It does not draft legal documents, interpret state law, or create an attorney-client relationship. Visitors should consult a qualified professional for advice about their situation.

Editorial standards

  • We avoid fake reviews, fake testimonials, and unverified product claims.
  • We do not present affiliate placeholders as endorsements or tested recommendations.
  • We separate educational guidance from monetization blocks.
  • We use plain English and clearly mark areas where rules can vary by state.
  • We encourage professional review for complex family, property, tax, business, special-needs, or conflict situations.

How sources are used

Estate planning is state-specific, so no general website can safely cover every legal requirement for every user. The source links below are used as background references and as places visitors can continue learning. They are not a substitute for legal advice.

Attorney review status

This version of the site has not been reviewed by an attorney. Do not add “attorney reviewed,” “expert approved,” or similar claims unless a qualified professional actually reviews the relevant content and you document that review.

Monetization policy

The site may include affiliate links, display ads, sponsored placements, newsletter forms, lead forms, or digital products. Affiliate links must use disclosure language near the link and should be marked with rel="sponsored nofollow". Monetization must not override accuracy or imply guaranteed legal outcomes.
