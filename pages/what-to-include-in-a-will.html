What to Include in a Will Planning Worksheet

A plain-English planning worksheet for decisions to discuss before drafting or updating a will.

Updated June 16, 2026 · General U.S. educational information

This page explains the kinds of decisions many people discuss when preparing a will. It is not a form, template, or substitute for legal drafting.

Identity and family information

Collect legal names, current addresses, former names if relevant, marital status, children, dependents, and family relationships that could affect planning.

Executor nomination

Decide who you would want to administer the estate and who should be named as alternate. Use the executor checklist for qualities and packet preparation.

Guardian nomination

Parents of minor children often use a will to nominate guardians. Because courts and state law matter, guardian nominations should be prepared carefully.

Specific gifts

Specific gifts may include sentimental items, family heirlooms, charitable gifts, or particular property. Make a list with clear descriptions and backup choices.

Remainder of the estate

After specific gifts, a will usually needs to address remaining property. This is an important drafting area, especially if beneficiaries, percentages, or backups are involved.

Special situations to flag

  • Minor beneficiaries.
  • Blended families or prior marriages.
  • Family conflict or disinheritance concerns.
  • Beneficiaries with disabilities or benefits planning concerns.
  • Business ownership.
  • Property in multiple states or countries.
  • Digital assets or cryptocurrency.
  • Charitable planning.

Signing requirements

Do not assume that printing and signing a document is enough. Witnesses, notarization, self-proving affidavits, electronic wills, and storage practices vary by state.

FAQ

Can a checklist tell me the exact wording to use in a will?

No. Wording should be prepared through a valid legal process for your state and circumstances.

What are residuary gifts?

A residuary clause generally addresses property not specifically given elsewhere. Ask a professional how it should be drafted.

Do witnesses or notarization matter?

Yes. Execution requirements vary by state and can affect validity. Confirm requirements before signing any document.
