Meeting prep worksheet

Prepare for an estate-planning conversation

A practical worksheet for organizing questions before speaking with a licensed estate planning professional or reputable planning service.

Updated June 16, 2026 · Meeting preparation worksheet

Privacy note: This worksheet is local to your browser page unless you copy or print it. Do not enter Social Security numbers, full account numbers, passwords, or other sensitive details.

Build a concise meeting brief

Use this before an attorney consultation, an online-service support call, or a family planning conversation.

Questions worth asking

  • What documents should I consider besides a will?
  • What signing, witness, or notarization steps apply in my state?
  • How should retirement accounts, life insurance, and transfer-on-death forms coordinate with the will?
  • What should my executor know now, and what should stay private?
  • Would a trust, power of attorney, or health directive be relevant to my situation?
  • How often should I review my documents after life changes?
  • How should originals and copies be stored?

Consultation request script

Use this as a starting point. Replace bracketed notes and do not include sensitive information until you know how the professional protects it.

Hello,

I am looking for help reviewing my estate planning options. I would like to discuss a will and related documents, including [brief family/assets context].

Could you please share whether you handle estate planning in my state, what the consultation process looks like, and what information I should prepare before a first meeting?

Thank you.

FAQ

Should I send sensitive details through a website form?

No. This page does not submit your information. Be careful with account numbers, passwords, Social Security numbers, and private family details.

What should I ask an estate planning attorney?

Ask about state requirements, signing steps, beneficiary forms, executor duties, guardianship, trusts, digital assets, updates, and document storage.

Is an attorney required to make a will?

Requirements vary and some people use other paths, but an attorney can provide advice tailored to your facts and state.
