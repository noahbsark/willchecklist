Digital Assets Checklist

Organize online accounts and access instructions without putting sensitive passwords in the wrong place.

Updated June 16, 2026 · General U.S. educational information

Digital assets can create confusion because they are invisible until someone needs them. The goal is to help trusted people know what exists and where instructions are stored without exposing passwords.

Digital asset inventory

  • Email accounts.
  • Password manager account and emergency access method.
  • Cloud storage, photos, and backup drives.
  • Banking, investing, insurance, and retirement portals.
  • Subscription services and recurring billing accounts.
  • Social media and creator accounts.
  • Domain names, websites, newsletters, or online businesses.
  • Crypto wallets or digital collectibles, if applicable.

Security-first rules

  • Do not put passwords in a will or public document.
  • Do not share seed phrases or recovery codes casually.
  • Use multi-factor authentication and document recovery methods safely.
  • Tell trusted people where the access process is stored, not necessarily the credentials themselves.
  • Keep the inventory current after changing devices or password managers.

Platform-specific planning

Some platforms offer legacy contacts, inactive account managers, memorialization options, or business account transfer procedures. Record which platforms matter and what settings you have configured.

Online business and creator accounts

If you earn money online, create a separate business continuity note. Include domain registrar, hosting, payment processors, email provider, marketplace accounts, tax documents, contractors, and renewal dates.

FAQ

Should I put passwords in my will?

Usually no. A will may become part of a court record. Use a secure password manager or another safe method and tell trusted people how access is handled.

What counts as a digital asset?

Email, financial logins, cloud files, photos, subscriptions, social media, domain names, business accounts, crypto wallets, and digital creative accounts may all matter.

Can an executor access every online account?

Access depends on law, platform policies, account settings, and planning documents. Avoid unsafe workarounds and ask a qualified professional for sensitive situations.
