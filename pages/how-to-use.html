Plain-English instructions

Use Will Checklist in the simplest possible way.

This page explains exactly what to click, what to type, what not to type, and how to know when you are done with a step.

Start the setupPrint quick-start sheet
1Use the setup first.

It asks one question at a time. Do not jump between every tool unless you already know what you need.

2Type labels, not secrets.

Use broad descriptions like “life insurance policy” or “safe in closet.” Never type passwords or full account numbers.

3Build a summary last.

After the checklist and locator, use the summary builder to create a high-level brief you can print or copy.

The easiest path

Do these 6 steps in order.

  1. Setup: answer one question at a time and follow the recommended next step. Open setup
  2. Start Here: answer the quick assessment so the site can suggest a path. Open Start Here
  3. Dashboard: check your progress and continue where you left off. Open dashboard
  4. Main Checklist: check off the planning categories you have gathered. Open checklist
  5. Document Locator: write where documents are stored, without codes or private numbers. Open locator
  6. Summary Builder: create a high-level planning brief. Open summary

What the buttons mean

Copy
Copies the visible summary text to your clipboard so you can paste it somewhere private.
Print
Opens your browser print dialog. You can print to paper or save as PDF.
Reset
Clears saved progress for that tool in this browser only. It does not affect other devices.
Export backup
Creates a local JSON backup from dashboard progress. Store it privately if you use it.

How progress is saved

Most tools save progress in your browser using local storage. That means the site does not need an account, but progress may not appear on another device or browser.

Private browsing, browser cleanup tools, or clearing site data may erase progress.

Avoid storing highly sensitive details. This site is an organizer, not a secure vault.

When to stop and ask a professional

Stop using the tools as your only guide and ask a licensed professional if you have a blended family, conflict among heirs, minor children, special-needs planning concerns, business ownership, tax questions, real estate in multiple states, or uncertainty about signing requirements.
