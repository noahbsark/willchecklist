Guardianship Checklist for Parents

A calm, practical worksheet for one of the hardest estate planning decisions.

Updated June 16, 2026 · General U.S. educational information

For parents and caregivers, guardianship questions can be emotional. A checklist helps separate practical facts from legal drafting so you can have clearer conversations.

Caregiving guardian considerations

  • Relationship with the child.
  • Willingness and ability to serve.
  • Parenting style, values, and stability.
  • Location, school, community, and family support.
  • Health, age, and household capacity.
  • Existing relationship with siblings and extended family.

Financial management considerations

The person best suited to raise a child may not be the best person to manage money. Discuss whether a trust, custodian, financial guardian, or other structure makes sense.

Name backups

Life changes. A backup guardian nomination can reduce uncertainty if your first choice cannot serve. Review names after moves, illness, divorce, conflict, or changing relationships.

Guardian letter worksheet

A non-legal letter can communicate routines and values:

  • Important medical, school, and activity contacts.
  • Family traditions and cultural or religious preferences.
  • Important relationships to maintain.
  • Pet care notes.
  • Education priorities.
  • Information about siblings staying together, if relevant.

When to get professional guidance

Get legal guidance if there are custody orders, blended family issues, special needs concerns, non-U.S. citizenship questions, substantial assets, expected disagreement, or multiple households involved.

FAQ

Does naming a guardian in a will guarantee the court will appoint that person?

Courts generally consider the child’s best interests and applicable law. Your nomination is important but does not eliminate court oversight.

Should I name the same person for money and caregiving?

Not always. Some families separate caregiving decisions from financial management. Ask an attorney about options.

Can I write a letter to a guardian?

A letter can be helpful for practical guidance, but legal nominations and financial structures should be prepared properly.
