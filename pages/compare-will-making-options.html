Comparison criteria

  • State coverage: Does the option address your state’s rules?
  • Complexity fit: Does it handle your family, assets, and goals?
  • Professional review: Is legal review included, optional, or unavailable?
  • Updates: Can you update after life changes?
  • Storage: What happens after signing, and where are originals kept?
  • Pricing: Is pricing clear before you enter sensitive information?
  • Privacy: How does the provider handle personal data?

Questions to ask before paying

  1. What situations are not covered?
  2. What happens if I make a mistake during signing?
  3. Can I speak with a licensed attorney in my state?
  4. How are updates handled?
  5. Will the service help with powers of attorney, health directives, or trusts if needed?

FAQ

Which will-making option is best?

There is no universal best option. Complexity, state law, budget, family situation, and risk tolerance all matter.

Are online will services legal?

Online services can be useful for some people, but validity and suitability depend on state requirements, the document, signing process, and your circumstances.

When should I use an attorney?

Consider an attorney when there are minors, blended families, conflict, business ownership, property in multiple states, special needs, tax questions, or uncertainty.
