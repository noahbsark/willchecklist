Beneficiary Designation Checklist

A practical checklist for finding beneficiary fields and reviewing them after life changes.

Updated June 16, 2026 · General U.S. educational information

Beneficiary designations are easy to overlook because they often live inside account portals, employer systems, and policy documents. This checklist helps you find and review them.

Accounts to check

  • Employer retirement plans.
  • Individual retirement accounts.
  • Life insurance policies.
  • Bank accounts with payable-on-death options.
  • Brokerage accounts with transfer-on-death registrations.
  • Health savings accounts and workplace benefits.
  • Annuities or other contracts with beneficiary fields.

What to review

  • Primary beneficiary names and contact details.
  • Backup or contingent beneficiaries.
  • Percentages that total correctly.
  • Outdated names after marriage, divorce, adoption, or death.
  • Beneficiaries who are minors or who may need protected planning.
  • Whether a trust, estate, charity, or individual is named and why.

Recordkeeping checklist

Create a beneficiary inventory that records the institution, account type, last review date, and where confirmation documents are stored. Avoid storing full account numbers in a shared spreadsheet.

Coordinate with your will

A will and beneficiary designations should be reviewed together. A mismatch can create confusion or outcomes you did not intend. Bring your inventory to an attorney or planning professional for review.

FAQ

Do beneficiary designations override a will?

Some assets may pass according to a beneficiary designation or account contract instead of a will. Ask a qualified professional how this works for your accounts.

How often should I review beneficiaries?

Review after major life events such as marriage, divorce, birth or adoption, death, separation, account changes, or moving states.

Should I list minor children directly?

Naming minors can create complications. Discuss guardian, trust, custodial, and backup options with a qualified professional.
