Executor Checklist

Choose and prepare an executor without leaving them a scavenger hunt.

Updated June 16, 2026 · General U.S. educational information

Choosing an executor is one of the most practical estate planning decisions. This guide helps you evaluate qualities, gather information, and prepare the details your chosen person may need later.

Executor qualities to consider

  • Trustworthiness and good judgment.
  • Ability to communicate with family members and professionals.
  • Organization and attention to paperwork.
  • Availability and willingness to serve.
  • Comfort handling deadlines, notices, and recordkeeping.
  • Location and ability to access necessary records, if relevant.

Questions to ask before naming someone

  1. Are they willing to serve if needed?
  2. Do they understand that the role may involve court procedures, professional help, and administrative work?
  3. Would conflicts among family members make the role difficult?
  4. Should a professional fiduciary or institution be discussed instead?
  5. Who should be named as backup?

Executor information packet

Your executor should not need to hunt for every basic fact. Prepare an information packet with:

  • Location of original documents.
  • Names and contact information for professional advisors.
  • Asset categories and record locations.
  • Insurance, retirement, banking, and employer benefit contacts.
  • Recurring bills and subscriptions.
  • Instructions for pets, property access, and important keys.
  • Digital asset inventory location, without exposed passwords.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Naming someone only because they are the oldest child.
  • Choosing a person who is likely to be overwhelmed by family conflict.
  • Failing to name a backup.
  • Leaving no clue where original documents are stored.
  • Assuming the executor automatically knows every account, debt, subscription, or benefit.
Practical tip: Your checklist can say where information is stored without listing sensitive passwords or complete account numbers in an insecure file.

FAQ

What is an executor?

An executor or personal representative is the person named to help administer an estate, subject to state law and court processes.

Should I tell the person I name?

It is usually wise to ask whether the person is willing, available, organized, and comfortable with the responsibility.

Can I name a backup?

Many plans include alternates in case the first choice cannot or will not serve. Ask a professional how to document this correctly.
