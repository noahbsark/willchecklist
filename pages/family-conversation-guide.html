Conversation prep

Plan the family conversation before documents become urgent.

Use gentle prompts to prepare what to say to a spouse, adult child, executor candidate, guardian candidate, or trusted family member.

Why this matters: a will checklist is more useful when the right people know that planning documents exist and where to find them. This guide helps you prepare a practical conversation without sharing more detail than you want to share.

Conversation guardrails

  • Ask for consent before assigning someone a role in your planning notes.
  • Keep the first conversation focused on responsibilities, location of documents, and willingness to help.
  • Avoid sending sensitive financial details by email or text.
  • Bring unresolved questions to a licensed estate planning professional.

FAQs

Should I tell my family everything in my will?

Not necessarily. This page helps you plan a practical conversation about roles and document location. What you disclose is personal and may depend on your family situation and professional advice.

Can this draft be used as a legal instruction?

No. Treat it as a communication aid only. Legal instructions should be handled through proper estate planning documents.
